An interactive live gameshow where the crowd plays along from their phones. No apps. No downloads. Just scan & guess.

Live Gameshow Crowd Participation 60–90 min
01 — The Concept

Think "4 Pics 1 Word" — but anime.

Four images go up on the big screen. Each one is a clue pointing to a single anime title. Groups come up to the stage one at a time, and they've got a ticking clock to figure it out. Meanwhile, the entire audience can play along from their phones by scanning a QR code — no app download needed, just a browser.

It's visual, it's competitive, it's loud, and it works whether someone's seen 10 anime or 1,000.

👀
Visually Driven
Perfect for big screens and projectors. The images do the talking — no trivia reading required.
📱
Crowd Play (Optional)
Attendees scan a QR code to submit guesses from their seats. Totally optional — zero friction.
📈
Scales Up Easily
Works for 30 people in a panel room or 3,000+ on a main stage. Same format, same energy.
🎭
Meme-Worthy Moments
Tricky image combos create hilarious reactions. Natural social media content.
02 — Show Format

How the show flows

Groups come up one at a time. The host reveals images, controls the timer, and awards points. Between groups, the crowd leaderboard can go up, polls can run, and the energy stays high. The whole thing runs about 60–90 minutes.

01
Opening
~10 minutes
Host intro, rules walkthrough, QR code goes up on the big screen for anyone who wants to play along from their phone.
02
Round 1 — Easy
~15–20 minutes
Iconic, well-known anime. Great warm-up. Think Naruto, Dragon Ball, Attack on Titan. Gets the crowd hyped.
03
Round 2 — Medium
~15–20 minutes
Trickier picks and more creative image angles. This is where it gets fun and people start second-guessing.
04
Round 3 — Hard
~15–20 minutes
Obscure titles, subtle visual cues, red herrings. This round separates the real ones from the casuals.
⚡
Finale — Lightning Round
~10–15 minutes
Rapid-fire questions. Top crowd players announced on stage. Grand winner crowned. Prizes go out.
03 — Crowd Engagement

Phones become controllers

The crowd participation is completely optional — anyone in the audience can scan a QR code displayed on the main screen and join a lightweight web app right in their browser. They see the same images, type their guess, and get scored on speed and accuracy. A live leaderboard keeps things competitive.

How It Works for Attendees
Scan the QR code → enter a display name → see the images on your phone → type your anime guess → submit before time runs out → check the leaderboard. That's it. No account, no download, no friction.
Frontend
Responsive web app. Works on any phone browser — iOS Safari, Android Chrome, desktop. Zero install.
Real-time
WebSocket connection for instant answer submission and live leaderboard updates.
Hosting
Cloud-hosted with auto-scaling. Handles hundreds to thousands of simultaneous players.
Admin
Host dashboard to advance rounds, reveal answers, trigger timers, and manage the question bank.
Latency
<200ms round-trip for answer submission. Leaderboard updates pushed in real-time.
🏆
Live Leaderboard
Top 10 on the big screen between rounds. Full rankings on each phone.
📊
Polls & Breaks
Quick audience polls between rounds to keep the energy flowing.
📸
Share Moments
Branded score screens for social media screenshots and bragging rights.
04 — Stage & Production

What we need on the day

The setup is straightforward. A screen to show images, a sound system, buzzers for the stage contestants, and internet for the crowd play system. Here's the breakdown:

🖥️
Display
LED screen or projector (16:9, 1080p minimum) for images, answers, and the crowd leaderboard.
🔊
Audio
Wireless mics for host + contestants. Sound system for anime OST clips and SFX stingers.
🔴
Buzzers
Physical or wireless buzzer system for on-stage contestants to lock in their answer.
🌐
Connectivity
Hardwired ethernet for admin systems. Attendees use venue Wi-Fi or mobile data.
Host / MC
Drives the energy, explains rules, interacts with contestants and crowd.
Tech Operator
Runs the admin dashboard — advances rounds, manages timers, monitors the web app.
AV Tech
Manages display, audio, and lighting cues throughout the show.
Stage Assists
Manage group flow on/off stage, distribute prizes, handle crowd logistics.
05 — Scalability

It works at any size

Same game, same format — just dial the production up or down. The web app auto-scales on cloud infrastructure so crowd participation works regardless of venue size.

S
30–100 people
Club, bar, or community meetup. Single screen. Intimate and loud.
M
100–500 people
Convention panel room. Dual screens recommended. Classic con energy.
L
500–5,000+
Main stage. Full production — LED wall, pro AV, dedicated Wi-Fi AP.
06 — Sponsors & Prizes

Natural integration, not interruption

The format has clean integration points that feel organic. Title sponsorship on all screens and the web app, individual round branding, prize sponsorship, and branded social media screenshot frames that attendees share themselves.

Prize Structure
Stage winners get the grand prize package — figures, manga, streaming subs, whatever sponsors provide. Top 3 crowd players get called up on stage for recognition and prizes. A random draw from all crowd participants keeps everyone engaged until the very end.
